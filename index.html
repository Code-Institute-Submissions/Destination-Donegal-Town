About Donegal Town

Donegal Town is the gateway to the Northwest. Donegal Town is the perfect location to explore the wonders of the Northwest. Ideally situated near the mouth of Donegal Bay, Donegal Town boasts a panoramic backdrop provided by the Blue Stack Mountains, a stately castle, a friendly population and an array of hotels and restaurants fit for every traveler and every taste. Donegal Town also boasts many clothing shops including world famous Magees of Donegal and The Irish House which are well worth a visit. There is plenty to do in Donegal Town and I have highlighted the top four recommendations for places to stay, kids activities, family activities, nightlife and places to eat below. On the banks of the River Eske, Donegal town was a stamping ground of the O'Donnells, the great chieftains who ruled the northwest from the 15th to 17th centuries. Today, despite being the county's namesake, it's neither its largest town (the much larger Letterkenny), nor the county town (that's the even smaller town of Lifford).

Donegal Town hosts a number of festivals throughout the year including the St Patricks Day Festival, the Donegal Town Summer Festival and A Taste of Donegal Food Festival....All these festivals are for the young and old and there is something for everyone. Please see social links on footer for further information

St Patrick's Day (Lá Fhéile Pádraig) is Ireland's national holiday and is held on 17th March every year. Donegal Town holds parade around the town starting at 3pm and consists of music, dance and colour. The parade attacts thousands to the town. There is plenty of kids activities on the diamond from 1pm and live music in all the pubs in town. Live music on stage on the Diamond from 5pm - 7pm. Spectacular Fireworks Display at Donegal Pier at 8pm. Donegal Town is the place to be on St Patricks Day

The Donegal Town Summer Festival is held on the first weekend of July annually. Live music on the diamond throughout the weekend from some of Ireland's most successful bands. Various activities all day every day to keep the young and old entertained including the Lady Nuala Final, Kids Fun Day, 10km run, kids tractor race, barmans challenge and soapbox derby to name but a few.

A Taste of Donegal Food Festival is held on the last weekend of August annually. This festival gathers Top Restaurants, Hotels, Food and Drink Providers. With 130 renowned exhibitors being showcased; you can meander, browse, meet the producers, smell, taste and purchase some of the finest food and drink available from all over the country. Cookery Demonstrations by high profile Celebrity Chefs including “Cherrymore Kids Kitchen”. Family Fun for all with a full entertainment programme throughout the weekend, Fireworks over Donegal Bay, Live music, Interactive Street Theatre. Come along and tantalise your taste buds.

Dgl Railway Centre

Donegal Railway Heritage Centre is considered one of the county’s hidden gems and is located just 300 metres from Donegal Town Centre. Whether you are a young family wanting to entertain the kiddos on a rainy day, a railway enthusiast or a person who loves to preserve local history. Donegal had the largest narrow-gauge railway network in the British Isles, and operated one of the first railway vehicles with an internal combustion engine in the world. Uniquely, this fascinating museum celebrating Donegal’s railway heritage, is based in one of the few remaining original station houses, which opened in 1889. The museum comprises of railway rolling stock, interesting artefacts, displays, a video presentation, model railways, a reference library, a shop and much more, including that famous Donegal welcome!

"Fairy" Bank Walk

If you cross Boyce's Bridge on the Killybegs road you will come across the beginning of the Bank Walk to your left. This 2.5 km walk is a delightful way to relax for an hour or two. The Bank Walk follows the west bank of the River Eske as it empties into Donegal Bay and offers a stunning view of the Old Abbey Green Island and Donegal Bay. The walk commences just past the stone bridge on the Mountcharles Road on the left. Along the walk, there are dozens of fairy doors for the kids to find. Throughout the year, it holds various events, includings the fairies Christmas lights, the Easter Egg hunt and the Spooky Bank walk held at Halloween.

Donegal Town Castle

Built by the O'Donnell chieftain in the 15th century, beside the River Eske, the Castle has extensive 17th century additions by Sir Basil Brooke. The Castle is furnished throughout and includes Persian rugs and French tapestries. Information panels chronicle the history of the Castle owners from the O'Donnell chieftains to the Brooke family. Limited access for people with disabilities to the ground floor. Location: In the Centre of Donegal Town. Guided Tours: Available every hour; Max. No: 35; Duration: 30 mins. Leaflet/Guide Booklet: English, Irish, French, German, Italian and Spanish.

Donegal Tours

A 4 and half hour bus tour of Donegal Town, Muckross Point, Slieve League Sea Cliffs, Glencolmcille and the Folk Village. Muckross Head is reached by a beautiful, winding, country road, neatly tucked into the mountainside, and overlooks an idyllic horse-shoe beach leading out to the wide Atlantic Ocean. Slieve League Sea Cliffs are said to be the highest and one of the finest marine cliffs in the whole of Europe, and with a six hundred metre drop down into the wild Atlantic waves below they create a breathtaking, spectacular view. Glencolmcille and the Folk Village gives a wonderful insight into life in this isolated part of rural Donegal over the past 200 years with it's beautiful collection of traditional thatched cottages.
